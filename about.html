Team Members

Lachlan Murray

Programmer

Currently the only developer at Corn Dog Games, Lachlan is working hard developing Umbra. From a very young age Lachlan has wanted to be a game developer and he is always looking to improve himself through searching for short courses, seeking out online tutorials, participating in game jams and constantly asking for feedback. Lachlan is primarily a programmer but isn't afraid to try any aspect of the field of game development.

Industry Friends

Michael Kalis

Designer

Michael is a great designer and programmer who is always thinking about the bigger picture. Always thinking about how to plan for and solve unexpected problems is a great skill of his. He has a great head on his shoulders and is a very helpful person. As a programming mentor and teacher Michael is among the best, going with the 'teach a man how to fish' approach.

Scott

"Pumpin Dog"

Scott is the sole developer at the games company 'Wampee', a company that makes crazy low poly 3D games with a lot of personality. You'll often find Scott talking about how he's trying to incorporate elements of Sonic or Bomberman into his games. Scott currently has 3 released games: Wampee Helicopters, Pumpkin Dog Islands, and Breeza Budgie Bill, with the plans to release more soon.

Michaela Taweel

Programmer

Michaela's part in working on 'Party Mayhem' was to develop the dodgeball minigame. It is a really fun and difficult replication of the real world game. Michaela is a driven developer and is great at problem solving; if there was ever a problem, Michaela would be straight on it trying to solve it. Michaela's work ethic, and team spirit was a driving force in getting 'Party Mayhem' to the point it reached.

Ashley Richmond

Programmer

Achley is also a very driven developer. Her part in developing 'Party Mayhem' was creating the Missile Madness minigame. Missile Madness was a crazy minigame where you have to avoid incoming missiles from all directions and be the last person standing. Ashley showed off not only great programming skill but also some very impressive pixel art capabilities.

Brandon Mawson

Programmer

Brandon was very enthuisiastic about the group project from the beginning, providing a lot of the key concepts that made it into the final game. The minigame he developed for 'Party Mayhem' was an interesting Pacman-like game. Even with lots of work to do at school and connection difficulties, he managed to play his part in making the game work. Brandon is overall a very nice person with a lot of potential in game development.

Harry Halliday

Programmer

Harry is a bit of a quite person at first but once you get to know him he has a lot to provide to a team. Harry developed a track racing game for 'Party Mayhem', with speed boosts and obstacles to make things interesting. This minigame has the potential to provide a ton of couch co-op fun for the whole family. Harry showed great project managmement skills being able to get such a complex minigame complete within the time given.

Thanks To

Christopher Larkin

Composer

Christopher Larkin kindly allowed the use of 2 of his tracks: 'Hornet' and 'Greenpath' in the Umbra trailer animation. He is a lovely person and composes the best orchestral music in recent history. His soundtracks immerse you in the world they help create and provide so much more to an atmosphere than the sum of their parts. Without these provided tracks, the Umbra animation wouldn't even be half of what it became. Christopher Larkin is truly a god of the composing world.
